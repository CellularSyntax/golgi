# Validation

golgi is validated layer by layer — the FEM solver against analytic and COMSOL references, the fiber
models against canonical electrophysiology, and end-to-end recruitment against published in-vivo and
cross-platform studies. The validation scripts live in
[`paper_figs/`](https://github.com/CellularSyntax/golgi/tree/main/paper_figs) (`validate_*.py`).

---

## What is checked against what

| Layer | Script(s) | Reference | Check |
|---|---|---|---|
| **FEM solver** | `validate_fem_analytic.py` | analytic point source `V(r)=I/(4πσ)(1/r−1/R)` | FEniCSx solution accuracy |
| **FEM (cross-platform)** | `comsol_validation_fig.py`, `_m1_golgi_cylinder.py` | **COMSOL** (M1 monopole, M2 idealized cuff w/ anisotropy + perineurium CI, M3 swine, M4 human) | point-for-point lead fields (S15) |
| **Conduction velocity** | `validate_fiber.py`, `validate_mrg.py` | **Hursh 1939**, **McIntyre 2002** (MRG) | CV-vs-diameter; MRG interpolation vs discrete |
| **Strength–duration** | `validate_fiber.py` | Weiss/Lapicque (rheobase, chronaxie) | threshold vs pulse width |
| **Dog cervical VNS** | `validate_dogvns_native.py` | **Yoo 2013** (in-vivo, via ASCENT) | A/B/C thresholds, LivaNova helical cuff, perineurium CI |
| **Cat tibial LIFE** | `validate_nrv*.py` | **NRV / Couppey 2024**, **Nannini-Horch 1991**, **Yoshida-Horch 1993** | intrafascicular recruitment |
| **Multi-fascicle cuff** | `validate_bucksot.py`, `fig_bucksot.py` | **Bucksot 2019** (via ASCENT) | per-fascicle recruitment, circumferential vs inverted cuff |

Digitized literature reference curves are stored as JSON (`save_bucksot_ref.py` →
`bucksot_reference.json`; `save_nrv_ref.py` → `nrv_reference.json`) for the overlays. The integrated
**Fig 4** combines the analytic/COMSOL, dog-VNS, NRV-LIFE, and Bucksot checks into one figure; **S8**
(`supp_foundations`) and **S15** (`supp_comsol_validation`) are the supplementary validation figures.
See [Reproducing the Paper](Reproducing-the-Paper).

---

## Why this matters

- The **analytic and COMSOL** checks establish that golgi's open FEniCSx solver matches both a closed
  form and the field most peripheral-nerve studies have historically used (COMSOL) — so dropping the
  commercial solver costs no accuracy. See [Finite-Element Solver](Finite-Element-Solver).
- The **Hursh / McIntyre** checks establish that the fiber backends reproduce textbook conduction
  velocity and strength–duration behaviour. See [Fiber Models & Activation](Fiber-Models-and-Activation).
- The **Yoo / Bucksot / NRV** checks establish end-to-end recruitment against independent in-vivo and
  cross-platform data, including the perineurium contact-impedance model. See
  [Conductivity & Tissue Properties](Conductivity-and-Tissue-Properties).

## Regression tests

Beyond the paper validation, the repository ships fast automated tests
([Contributing](Contributing)): source/attribute guards that the headless API stays wired to its
pipeline drivers, a full `import_nerve → run_mesh → run_fem → run_fibers → run_sweep` integration test
(auto-skips without the solver stack), and pure-numpy unit tests of the
[CNAP cable math](Recording-and-CNAP).

## References

**Fiber-model foundations**
- Hursh JB (1939). Conduction velocity and diameter of nerve fibers. *Am J Physiol* 127:131.
- McIntyre CC, Richardson AG, Grill WM (2002). Modeling the excitability of mammalian nerve fibers. *J Neurophysiol* 87:995.
- Weiss G (1901). Sur la possibilité de rendre comparables entre eux les appareils servant à l'excitation. *Arch Ital Biol* 35:413. — strength–duration.
- Lapicque L (1907). Recherches quantitatives sur l'excitation électrique des nerfs. *J Physiol Pathol Gén* 9:620. — chronaxie.

**FEM cross-validation**
- Bossetti CA, Birdno MJ, Grill WM (2008). Analysis of the quasi-static approximation for calculating potentials generated by neural stimulation. *J Neural Eng* 5:44.
- Pelot NA, Thio BJ, Grill WM (2019). On the parameters used in finite element modeling of compound peripheral nerves. *J Neural Eng* 16:016007.
- COMSOL Multiphysics (commercial; used only as an external cross-check, not by golgi).

**End-to-end recruitment**
- Yoo PB, et al. (2013). High-resolution measurement of electrically-evoked vagus nerve activity. *J Neural Eng.* — dog cervical VNS.
- Bucksot JE, et al. (2019). Flat electrode contacts for vagus nerve stimulation. *PLOS ONE.* — multi-fascicle cuff.
- Nannini N, Horch K (1991). Muscle recruitment with intrafascicular electrodes. *IEEE Trans Biomed Eng.* — LIFE.
- Yoshida K, Horch K (1993). Selective stimulation of peripheral nerve fibers using dual intrafascicular electrodes. *IEEE Trans Biomed Eng.* — LIFE.
- Couppey T, et al. (2024). NRV: an open framework for in silico evaluation of peripheral nerve stimulation. *PLOS Comput Biol.* — cross-platform LIFE.
- Musselman ED, Cariello JE, Grill WM, Pelot NA (2021). ASCENT: a pipeline for sample-specific computational modeling of electrical stimulation of peripheral nerves. *PLOS Comput Biol.* — reference masks/pipeline for the dog-VNS and Bucksot checks.
